Juniper's Network Redesign

This page was last updated on July 19, 2026.

The VIA Better Bus Plan is currently underway and is set to be fully implemented before 2030. For the most part, I am in favor of the majority of the changes that VIA intends to make to the system. However, there are still some things that I personally feel could be improved, particularly when it comes to bringing new service to areas that haven't seen bus service in years, if at all.

This page documents my attempt at redesigning San Antonio's transit system, with a priority on realistic proposals that could happen after the Better Bus plan is complete.

I also don't want to imply that I know how to make a better system than the people at VIA who have degrees in this field (I am just an unemployed college dropout after all). Note that some of my proposals are more pipe-dreamy than anything (after all, while it would be awesome for us to have system-wide frequencies be 15 minutes or less and have access to bus service within a 10 minute walk, that just simply isn't feasable with VIA's severely inadequate funding).

Interactive map

The map below is interactable and can be used to view the entire system at once. To open the map in a new tab, simply click the "fullscreen" button in the top right corner of the embedded map.

The green dots are express stops, and show the locations where Skip, Express, and Prímo buses would stop. The purple dots are local stops. The only local stops that are visible on this map are stops that do not exist in real life (for example, my proposal for Route 1 Lockhill-Selma has stops labeled along Lockhill-Selma between 1604 and Huebner and within Shavano Highlands, but not on any other road since those other roads have stops that exist already for other existing routes).
