Updated schedule alert

If you downloaded the 202 or 225 schedules before January 15, 2026, those have received some corrections. In addition, a brand new timetable for Route 97's late-night service has been created. You can download the updated schedules below.

Lineup is a service that VIA operates every night, with buses departing the Downtown Service Hub (near Travis Park) at 10:30pm, 11:30pm, and 12:30am to give riders a final chance to make transfers and reach their final destinations. VIA, however, does not provide dedicated timetables for lineup routes, instead scattering the times across their regular daytime timetables. This page contains custom timetables that I have made for routes 202â€“289 in the style of their daytime schedules as a convenient alternative to VIA's brochures.

When VIA announces service changes that affect lineup scheduling, new timetables will be uploaded here as fast as I can create them.

Long-term service note

The VIA Better Bus plan seems to call for lineup service to be completely eliminated by 2030 in favor of extending the service hours of daytime routes to 12:30am.


If passed as it is currently proposed, then on January 12, 2026, Route 209 Broadway / Nacogdoches Lineup will be eliminated due to low ridership on Broadway between Nacogdoches and Loop 410; however, Route 243 South Flores / Pleasanton Lineup and Route 296 Vance Jackson / West Avenue Lineup will be eliminated in favor of Route 43 & 44 and 96 & 97's service hours being extended to 12:30am to replace their respective lineup services.

Printable Schedules

Would you like to have a physical copy of a particular schedule to bring with you while you're out and about? Using one of the download links below, you can do that very easily! Because the actual timetables are so short, all lineup schedules fit on a single piece of US letter paper (8.5 x 11 inches).

The printable copies of the schedules have lines outside of the actual content area. The solid lines are fold lines: just fold the paper along those lines. The broken (or dashed) lines are cut lines: after you fold the schedule, just take a pair of scissors and cut along those lines.

A quirk you'll notice about the printable schedules is that the second page (for the backside) is upside down. This is intentional by design; if you unfold and look at an official pocket schedule from VIA, you'll notice that they share the exact same quirk: the backside with the Saturday and Sunday schedules is upside down. This was done to ensure that the cover page is directly behind the map.

Ink-saving schedules

The black-and-white schedules have been discontinued as the color schedules will print just fine when using a printer that only prints in grayscale. These will be replaced by a new type of printable schedule: Ink-saving schedules.

Ink-saving schedules are exactly what they sound like: they're schedules that are created with the goal of using as little ink as possible (since after all, the main point of timetables is to provide information to riders, and not to be pretty).

These will be created at some point in the future; I just have a long backlog of other timetables that I am prioritizing over these.
